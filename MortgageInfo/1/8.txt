-> Extension vs. Re-Lock

### 1. Extension = Before Expiration

* **Definition:** An extension occurs **before** the currently agreed-upon rate lock period has officially ended.
* **Purpose:** The borrower needs more time to close the loan (e.g., waiting for an appraisal or title work), and they want to keep the current locked interest rate they already secured.
* **Action:** The lender grants a short, specific extension of the existing rate (e.g., 7 or 15 days).
* **Cost:** Extensions usually come with a **fee** paid by the borrower to the lender to compensate the lender for holding the rate commitment longer.

### 2. Re-Lock = After Expiration

* **Definition:** A re-lock occurs **after** the previous rate lock period has completely expired and the borrower is currently "unlocked" (floating in the market).
* **Purpose:** The loan still hasn't closed, and the borrower must secure a new, current interest rate to proceed.
* **Action:** The loan is re-locked at the **current market rate** at the time of the re-lock, not the previous rate.
* **Cost:** Re-locks often involve a **fee** and may result in the borrower securing a higher or lower rate, depending on how the market has moved since the original lock expired.

**In summary:**

* **Extension:** You are *extending* the life of your **original**, favorable rate.
* **Re-Lock:** You are *locking* in a **new** rate because the old one is gone.
