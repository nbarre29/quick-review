-> Loan Programs

In mortgage terminology, names like:

* **Jumbo**
* **Jumbo Manual**
* **Jumbo AUS**
* **Agency Enhanced**

are indeed examples of **loan programs** (sometimes also called **loan products** depending on your company‚Äôs internal naming convention).

---

### üîπ Here‚Äôs what that means in context

Each of those terms represents a **distinct set of lending rules and eligibility criteria** that define *how* the loan is processed, priced, and underwritten.

| Loan Program        | Meaning / Key Features                                                                                                                                                                |
| ------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Jumbo**           | A loan amount that exceeds conforming loan limits set by Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac. These are funded and held by private investors.                                                  |
| **Jumbo Manual**    | A **Jumbo loan** that is **manually underwritten** ‚Äî i.e., an underwriter reviews credit, income, and assets instead of relying on an automated system.                               |
| **Jumbo AUS**       | A **Jumbo loan** that uses an **Automated Underwriting System (AUS)** for eligibility (like DU or LP). Some investors allow this for streamlined processing.                          |
| **Agency Enhanced** | A **conforming loan** that follows **Fannie Mae/Freddie Mac (‚Äúagency‚Äù)** guidelines but with **enhanced features** ‚Äî e.g., higher LTVs, expanded DTI, or special pricing adjustments. |

---

### üîπ Why they are considered Loan Programs

Each of these:

* Defines **loan limits**, **credit score cutoffs**, **LTV/DTI caps**, and **rate sheets**
* Is selectable in pricing or underwriting systems (like Optimal Blue, Encompass, or your internal pricing engine)
* Drives logic for eligibility, pricing, and documentation requirements

-> JPQM30 and JPQM15 

Excellent question ‚Äî and you‚Äôre clearly thinking like someone building or interpreting a **mortgage pricing or eligibility engine** üëè

Let‚Äôs decode those two terms:

---

### üîπ **JPQM30** and **JPQM15**

These are **specific loan program codes** (or product codes) used *within the Jumbo Manual loan family*.

They typically stand for:

| Code       | Expanded Name                                        | Typical Meaning                                                                                          |
| ---------- | ---------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **JPQM30** | **Jumbo Program Qualified Mortgage ‚Äì 30 Year Fixed** | A **Jumbo Manual** loan that meets **Qualified Mortgage (QM)** rules, with a **30-year fixed rate term** |
| **JPQM15** | **Jumbo Program Qualified Mortgage ‚Äì 15 Year Fixed** | A **Jumbo Manual** loan that meets **QM** rules, with a **15-year fixed rate term**                      |

---

### üîπ Breaking It Down

Let‚Äôs unpack the naming convention piece by piece:

| Part        | Meaning                                                                                           |
| ----------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **J**       | Jumbo (the overall loan category)                                                                 |
| **P**       | Program (sometimes stands for ‚ÄúProduct‚Äù or ‚ÄúProgram Type‚Äù)                                        |
| **QM**      | Qualified Mortgage ‚Äî meaning it meets the CFPB‚Äôs *Ability-to-Repay* and *QM* safe harbor criteria |
| **30 / 15** | Loan term in years (30-year or 15-year fixed)                                                     |

So:
**JPQM30 = Jumbo Program (Manual) Qualified Mortgage, 30-year fixed**
**JPQM15 = Jumbo Program (Manual) Qualified Mortgage, 15-year fixed**

-> JPQM30, JPQM15, JPQM56, JPQM76, JPQM106

Those codes ‚Äî **JPQM30, JPQM15, JPQM56, JPQM76, JPQM106** ‚Äî are **loan program codes** (or **product codes**) used internally by lenders or pricing engines, especially for **Jumbo Non-QM loan programs**. Let‚Äôs break them down:

---

### üîπ General Pattern:

**JPQMxx**

* **J** ‚Üí Jumbo
* **PQM** ‚Üí likely stands for *Prime / Private / Portfolio Qualified Mortgage* (or *Jumbo Prime Qualified Mortgage*) ‚Äî it refers to **Non-QM or near-prime Jumbo loans**.
* **xx** ‚Üí the **number** usually represents the **loan term or program type**.

---

### üîç Likely Meaning of Each Code:

| Code        | Likely Meaning                     | Explanation                                                                                                                                                                        |
| ----------- | ---------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **JPQM30**  | Jumbo Prime / Non-QM 30-Year Fixed | A **30-year fixed-rate Jumbo Non-QM** loan program. Typically for borrowers who don‚Äôt meet standard agency (Fannie/Freddie) criteria but qualify under lender-specific guidelines. |
| **JPQM15**  | Jumbo Prime / Non-QM 15-Year Fixed | A **15-year fixed-rate Jumbo Non-QM** loan program. Shorter term, lower rate, higher monthly payment.                                                                              |
| **JPQM56**  | Jumbo Prime / Non-QM 5/6 ARM       | A **5-year adjustable-rate mortgage** where rate adjusts every 6 months after initial 5 years. (‚Äú5/6‚Äù = 5 years fixed, adjusts every 6 months.)                                    |
| **JPQM76**  | Jumbo Prime / Non-QM 7/6 ARM       | A **7-year ARM**, fixed for 7 years, adjusts every 6 months afterward.                                                                                                             |
| **JPQM106** | Jumbo Prime / Non-QM 10/6 ARM      | A **10-year ARM**, fixed for 10 years, adjusts every 6 months thereafter. Most stable ARM option among the three.                                                                  |

---

### üí° Context:

These codes typically appear in:

* **Rate sheets / Pricing Engines** (e.g., Optimal Blue, EPPS, Polly)
* **Lock Desk systems**
* **Loan origination systems (LOS)** under the ‚ÄúLoan Program‚Äù dropdown

They define what type of **product** the borrower is locking ‚Äî in this case, a **Jumbo Non-QM loan**, either **fixed** or **ARM** (adjustable).

---

### üè† Summary:

All of these are **Jumbo Non-QM loan programs**, where:

* ‚ÄúJPQM‚Äù = Jumbo Prime/Non-QM
* The number indicates the **term and ARM structure**:

  * `30` ‚Üí 30-year fixed
  * `15` ‚Üí 15-year fixed
  * `56` ‚Üí 5/6 ARM
  * `76` ‚Üí 7/6 ARM
  * `106` ‚Üí 10/6 ARM
